What I Loathe

There is nothing I loathe more, with the exception of possibly Nazi’s and pedophiles, than rushing to finish a story, and not giving it the time it deserves to become the best story possible. It’s why I often take longer in-between novels than many other authors. I’m not interested in just cranking stories out to make the paycheck, even though my publisher would probably be overjoyed if I were to change my tune. It’s an important truth to me, that to create work that will be lasting and meaningful, if even only to myself, that I must willingly sacrifice my time to the Muse. The greatest tales are the most inspired, and inspiration comes from being willing to work. Deadlines are positive motivators, but when the deadline begins to outweigh the importance of quality, then my ire gets turned up to eleven.
